Top 8 Trends in Exterior Stone Cladding for 2023


Exterior stone cladding uses natural or engineered stone to cover the exterior walls of a building. This technique has been used for centuries and has evolved to include various options that fit any architectural style. The use of exterior stone cladding has become increasingly popular in recent years due to its durability, aesthetic appeal, and ability to increase property value. As with any design element, keeping up with current trends is essential to ensure that your home or building looks modern and up-to-date. This blog post will discuss the top trends in exterior stone cladding for 2023, including bold colors and patterns, textured stone, sustainable and eco-friendly options, mixed materials, large stone panels, stone veneer, and minimalist design. By the end of this post, you will better understand what's trending in exterior stone cladding and how you can incorporate these trends into your home or building design.

What is Exterior Stone Cladding?

Exterior stone cladding refers to the practice of covering the exterior walls of a building with stone materials such as natural stone, cultured stone, or stone veneer. This technique is commonly used in residential and commercial construction projects to provide an aesthetically pleasing and durable finish to the building envelope.


The process of exterior stone cladding typically involves attaching stone panels or tiles to the outer walls of a building using adhesive or mechanical fasteners. The stone materials used for external cladding can vary widely in size, shape, texture, and color, allowing for a high degree of customization and flexibility in design.


One of the primary benefits of using stone for exterior cladding is its durability and resistance to weathering and damage from the elements. Stone materials are also fire-resistant and provide a natural insulation barrier, helping to improve the energy efficiency of the building.


In addition to its functional benefits, exterior stone cladding can also enhance the visual appeal of a building, creating a striking and elegant appearance that can help the building stand out in its surroundings. From classic, traditional designs to modern and contemporary styles, exterior stone cladding offers a wide range of aesthetic options for architects, designers, and property owners alike.

Top 8 Trends in Exterior Stone Cladding

Trend 1: Large Format Stone Panels

Large format stone panels, also known as oversized stone panels, are a growing trend in exterior cladding. These panels are made of natural or engineered stone and are much larger than traditional stone tiles or slabs. Typically, large format stone panels are around 5 feet by 10 feet but can be even more significant. This trend has gained popularity in recent years because of its ability to create a sleek, modern look for buildings.


One of the main benefits of using large format stone panels for exterior cladding is their ability to cover a large area with minimal seams. This creates a seamless, uniform look that is very aesthetically pleasing. Additionally, because the panels are so large, installation time can be reduced, which can save money on labor costs.

Trend 2: Sustainable Materials

Sustainable materials are becoming increasingly important in all areas of design, including exterior stone cladding. Sustainable materials have been sourced and manufactured to minimize their impact on the environment. This includes materials made from recycled content, are rapidly renewable, or have a low carbon footprint. Using sustainable materials can have many benefits when it comes to exterior stone cladding.


One of the primary benefits of using sustainable materials for exterior cladding is that it reduces the environmental impact of a building. Using materials with a low carbon footprint or that have been recycled can reduce the amount of energy needed to manufacture the materials and the amount of waste generated during the manufacturing process. Additionally, sustainable materials can often be reused or repurposed at the end of their life, reducing waste.


Trend 3: Textured Stone Finishes

Textured stone finishes are a trend in exterior stone cladding that adds depth and dimension to a building's façade. Textured finishes can be achieved through various methods, including bush-hammering, chiseling, or sandblasting the stone's surface. This trend has gained popularity because of its ability to add interest and character to a building's exterior.


One of the primary benefits of using textured stone finishes for exterior cladding is that it creates a tactile and visual interest in the building's façade. Adding texture to the stone creates a unique, eye-catching look that separates your structure. Additionally, textured finishes can help disguise any imperfections in the stone and make the surface less slippery in wet conditions.


Trend 4: Contrasting Colors

Contrasting colors in exterior cladding refer to using different colors and tones of stone to create a visual contrast on a building's façade. This trend has gained popularity because of its ability to add interest and depth to a building's exterior.


One of the primary benefits of using contrasting colors for exterior cladding is that it can make a building stand out and create a bold visual statement. Contrasting colors can highlight architectural details or create a dynamic interplay between different materials and surfaces. Additionally, contrasting colors can help to break up large expanses of stone and add interest to what would otherwise be a monochromatic surface.


Trend 5: Geometric Shapes

Geometric shapes in exterior cladding refer to using different shapes and patterns in stone to create a visually exciting and dynamic façade. This trend has gained popularity because it creates a modern and contemporary look on a building's exterior.


One of the primary benefits of using geometric shapes for exterior cladding is that it can create a unique and eye-catching design that sets a building apart. Geometric shapes can be used to highlight architectural features or create a repeating pattern on the stone's surface. Additionally, geometric shapes can help to break up large expanses of stone and add interest to what would otherwise be a monotonous surface.


Trend 6: Thin Stone Veneers

Thin stone veneers refer to the exterior cladding that utilizes thin slices of natural stone, typically 1-2 inches thick. This trend has gained popularity due to its cost-effectiveness, sustainability, and versatility in design.


One of the primary benefits of using thin stone veneers for exterior cladding is that they offer a cost-effective alternative to traditional stone cladding. Thin stone veneers require less material and labor for installation, resulting in lower costs. Additionally, thin stone veneers are more environmentally sustainable than thicker stone cladding options because they need less material extraction and transportation.


Thin stone veneers also offer a wide range of design possibilities. They can be cut into various shapes and sizes, allowing for intricate and unique designs. Thin stone veneers can also be used to create a variety of textures and patterns, such as stacked or staggered patterns.


Trend 7: Rustic and Natural Stone

Rustic and natural stone is a trend in exterior cladding involving raw and unrefined stone to create a natural and authentic look. This trend is gaining popularity due to its timeless appeal and ability to blend seamlessly with natural surroundings.


One of the primary benefits of using rustic and natural stone for exterior cladding is that it creates a sense of harmony between the building and its environment. In addition, using natural stone helps create a visual connection between the building and its surroundings, making it appear as if the building is a natural part of the landscape.


Another benefit of using rustic and natural stone for exterior cladding is that it provides a timeless and classic look. Unlike other trends that may come and go, natural stone has been famous for centuries and will continue to be a popular choice for many years.


Trend 8: Mixed Materials

Mixed materials are a trend in exterior cladding involving multiple materials to create a unique and modern look. This trend has become increasingly popular as architects and designers seek to create distinctive and eye-catching building façades.


One of the primary benefits of using mixed materials for exterior cladding is that it allows for a high degree of creativity and customization. By combining different materials, designers can create a wide range of textures, colors, and patterns that can be tailored to each project's specific needs and preferences.


Another benefit of using mixed materials for exterior cladding is that it can help to improve the durability and performance of the building envelope. For example, using a combination of stone and metal can create a façade that is both aesthetically pleasing and highly durable, with the stone providing a solid base and the metal providing additional protection against the elements.


The top trends in exterior stone cladding for 2023 offer designers and property owners various options to create visually stunning and durable building facades. From large format panels to mixed materials and natural stone, there are numerous possibilities for customization and creative expression. Keeping up with these trends is essential for aesthetic reasons and sustainability and energy efficiency considerations. By implementing these trends in future exterior cladding projects, architects and designers can create buildings that are not only beautiful but also functional and environmentally responsible. So why not consider incorporating these top trends in your next exterior cladding project and make a bold statement with your building design?
